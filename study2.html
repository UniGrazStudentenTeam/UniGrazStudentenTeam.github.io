The authors created and validated an anxiety level prediction technology, based on the data from a wristband (blood volume pressure, galvanic skin response, body temperature) meant to be used in VR Exposure therapy. They trained and evaluated their model on 30 participants undergoing a Virtual Reality Exposure treatment for public speaking.

Anxiety Level Recognition for Virtual Reality Therapy System Using Physiological Signals

What was done?

Development of a wearable that unobtrusiveley and continuously captures details of behavours and context to provide coues about anxiety onset

What can be concluded?

The paper was a proposal of a platform and approach for the analysis of human physical and vocal activities in association with their anxiety. Severeal features of ativity and audio data that most relate to anxiety state has been found. A non intrusive and continuously way to collect acoustic and physical activity was designed and executed.
