EEG Headband anxiety detection

Short Overview

A study on multi-class anxiety detection using wearable EEG headband



In this study Arsalan & Majid (2021) presented an anxiety detection framework based on the EEG (Electroencephalography) data collected from 65 participants. The study describes in detail the data acquisition, pre-processing, feature extraction and selection, and classification stages. Two approaches were developed: for the binary(anxious/non-anxious) and multi-class(non-anxious/low-anxious/high-anxious) classification.



After performing channel selection by using the t test (2 classes) and ANOVA (3 classes) TP9 and AF8 were found to be statistically significantly different channels. From those channels 5 time-domain features were selected for further classifier training. Out of Random Forest, Logistic Regression and Multilayer perceptron, Random Forest classifier yielded the best accuracy score for both 2 and 3 class problems, 87.69 and 83.07, respectively.



When working with the EEG data it is crucial to perform thorough channel and feature selection. This will yield better results later on during the classifier training. The developed pipeline is able to differentiate not only between anxious and non-anxious classes, but also to distinguish the level of anxiousness as well. Authors demonstrated that it is possible to provide a classification pipeline for anxiety using a low-cost device with only four channels. This setup can be integrated into daily life, not just in clinical settings.
