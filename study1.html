The authors created and validated an anxiety level prediction technology, based on the data from a wristband (blood volume pressure, galvanic skin response, body temperature) meant to be used in VR Exposure therapy. They trained and evaluated their model on 30 participants undergoing a Virtual Reality Exposure treatment for public speaking.

After various preprocessing steps, they were able to achieve a classification accuracy of 80.1% (leave one out) or 86.3% (10x10 fold cross validation) with the Support Vector Machine classifier. Furthermore they found that a 20s window length for the prediction yielded the highest accuracy.

VR Exposure Therapy Systems can be used together with AI wearables that predict anxiety during the treatment with a considerable accuracy. This can in the future help to improve VR treatments to become more personalised and adaptive.
